Ye (born Kanye Omari West on June 8, 1977; Atlanta, June 8, 1977) is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, music producer, politician, and fashion designer. He gained prominence as a producer at Roc-A-Fella Records, working on Jay-Zâ€™s album The Blueprint and hits for artists like Alicia Keys, Ludacris, and Janet Jackson. With 24 Grammys, he is the most awarded rapper in history and the seventh most awarded artist in all editions of the awards. Ye has released 11 solo albums, including The College Dropout (2004), My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy (2010), Yeezus (2013), and Donda (2021), as well as collaborative projects such as Watch the Throne with Jay-Z and Kids See Ghosts with Kid Cudi. He owns the label G.O.O.D. Music and created the Yeezy brand in partnership with Adidas, also collaborating with brands like Nike, Louis Vuitton, and Balenciaga. His musical style has evolved over the years, incorporating influences from soul, R&B, rock, electronic music, and classical music. Ye is also known for his Christian faith, his public controversies, his independent presidential campaign in 2020, and his work as a fashion designer. He has sold over 160 million records worldwide, solidifying himself as one of the best-selling and most awarded artists of all time.
