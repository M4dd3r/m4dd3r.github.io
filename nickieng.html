Nicki Minaj (Onika Tanya Maraj), born on December 8, 1982, is a rapper, singer, songwriter, model, and actress from Trinidad and Tobago, based in the United States. She moved to Queens, New York, at the age of five and, after releasing mixtapes between 2007 and 2009, signed with the Young Money label. Her debut album, *Pink Friday* (2010), was a major success, topping the Billboard 200 and receiving platinum certification. The single "Super Bass" became one of the best-selling songs in the US. In 2012, her second album, *Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded*, also achieved success, with the hit "Starships" standing out. Minaj is known for her extravagant aesthetics, use of alter egos, and fast-paced rap, as well as becoming a fashion icon. She also lent her voice to *Ice Age 4*, served as a judge on *American Idol*, and partnered with brands such as MAC Cosmetics and Pepsi. Minaj has received numerous awards, including 11 BET Awards, 8 BET Hip-Hop Awards, and 6 American Music Awards, and was named "Rising Star" by Billboard in 2011. She has been nominated for the Grammy Awards 9 times and became the first woman to achieve 100 entries on the Billboard Hot 100, cementing her place as one of the most influential rappers of all time.
