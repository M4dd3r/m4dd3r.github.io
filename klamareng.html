Kendrick Lamar (Compton, June 17, 1987) is an American rapper, songwriter, and producer, widely regarded as one of the greatest artists of his generation and in hip-hop history. Winner of 17 Grammys and the Pulitzer Prize for Music, he has stood out for his lyrical depth and musical innovation. He began his career with the mixtape Overly Dedicated (2010) and released Section.80 (2011), gaining initial attention. His second album, Good Kid, M.A.A.D City (2012), established him as a rap powerhouse, featuring hits like "Swimming Pools (Drank)". To Pimp a Butterfly (2015) was a cultural milestone, blending jazz, funk, and rap, while Damn (2017) earned him the Pulitzer and global acclaim. Lamar also curated the soundtrack for the movie Black Panther (2018), collaborating with various artists. He is known for addressing social, racial, and identity themes, maintaining great relevance in contemporary music and culture.
