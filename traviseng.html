Jacques Berman Webster II (born April 30, 1991, in Houston), known artistically as Travis Scott, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and music producer. Scott signed his first major contract in 2012 with Epic Records. In the same year, he joined Kanye West's GOOD Music label as a producer on the collaborative album Cruel Summer. In 2013, he also signed with T.I.'s Grand Hustle Records, solidifying his position in the music industry. His debut project was the mixtape Owl Pharaoh, released in 2013, followed by Days Before Rodeo in 2014. In September 2015, Scott released his first studio album, Rodeo, which included the hit "Antidote", reaching the Billboard Hot 100 top 20. His second album, Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight (2016), was critically acclaimed. In 2017, he collaborated with Quavo on the album Huncho Jack, Jack Huncho, signed by the duo as Huncho Jack. His third album, Astroworld, was released on August 3, 2018, further solidifying his career.
